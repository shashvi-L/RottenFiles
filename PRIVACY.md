Privacy Policy (last updated: June 2026)

RottenFiles is a local Windows utility that does not collect, store, transmit, or share any personal information with the developer or any third party.

What the app accesses: RottenFiles reads file names, file paths, and file creation/modification timestamps within the folder you configure it to watch on your own device. This information is used solely to track expiry dates and display them within the app's interface.

Where this data is stored: All file tracking data is stored locally on your device in a local database file. It is never transmitted over the internet, uploaded to any server, or shared with the developer, Microsoft, or any other party.

What the app does not do: RottenFiles does not collect analytics, telemetry, usage statistics, or any identifying information about you or your device. It does not require an account, login, or internet connection to function. It does not access files outside the folder you specify.

File deletion: Files that exceed their configured expiry period are permanently deleted from your device by the app, based on settings you control. This action happens locally and is not logged or reported anywhere outside your device.

Changes to this policy: If RottenFiles' data practices change in a future update, this policy will be updated accordingly and the new version will be reflected in the app's GitHub repository.
Contact: For questions about this policy, open an issue on the RottenFiles GitHub repository: https://github.com/shashvi-L/RottenFiles
